Lady Triệu

Việt Nam Sử Lược (A Brief History of Vietnam), a history book that was written in the early 20th century by Vietnamese historian Trần Trọng Kim, said the following about Lady Triệu:

  • Lady Triệu was a native of Nông Cống district
  • When Chinese state of Eastern Wu occuppied Vietnam, Lady Triệu went to the mountain and gathered a band of 1.000 followers
  • Her brother tried to persuade her from rebelling, she told him:

    • "I only want to ride the wind and walk the waves, slay the big whales of the Eastern sea, clean up frontiers, and save the people from drowning. Why should I imitate others, bow my head, stoop over and be a slave? Why resign myself to menial housework?"

  • When she went to battles, she usually wore yellow tunics and rode a war-elephant.
  • Lady Triệu had managed to fight back the Wu forces for 5 or 6 months. Because of the lack of troops, she could not manage to fight a long war and was defeated.
  • She fled to Bồ Điền commune and then committed suicide.
